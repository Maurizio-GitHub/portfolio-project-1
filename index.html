The few things you really need to know about

NFT stands for Non-Fungible Token, a special type of non-interchangeable encrypted token representing a unique and inimitable virtual object of its kind.

For this reason, NFTs have effectively become the best tool to transfer digital works from one person to another, without having to transfer rights via old methods related to copyright.

A meme, an image, a video, a game or even a tweet can be sold through NFTs. This unequivocally certifies the ownership of the idea behind any digital content. Therefore, NFTs are also identified as digital certificates: they represent the deed of ownership and certify the authenticity of an asset.


Blockchain, a shared and immutable digital ledger where transaction data is stored and cannot be altered or deleted, plays a very important role in the process of NFTs creation.

A specific sequence of numbers is, indeed, associated to each and every digital work in order to ensure, over time, traceability and immutability of the token itself. This algorithmically-generated code, called hash, is registered on a blockchain via asmart contract.

Finally, the token can become an object to exchange on dedicated marketplaces.
