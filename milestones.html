  • May 2014

    The Beginning

    Kevin McCoy and Anil Dash create 'Quantum', the first known NFT. It consists of a video clip made by McCoy's wife, Jennifer. McCoy registered the video on the Namecoin blockchain and sold it to Dash for $4, during a live presentation for the Seven on Seven conference at the New Museum in New York City. McCoy and Dash referred to the technology as 'monetized graphics'. This explicitly linked a non-fungible, tradable blockchain marker to a work of art, via on-chain metadata (enabled by Namecoin).

  • October 2015

    The Ethereum Blockchain

    The first NFT project, Etheria, is launched and demonstrate in London at DEVCON 1, Ethereum's first developer conference, three months after the launch of the Ethereum blockchain.

  • December 2017

    the Online Gaming

    The term NFT achieves wider usage with the ERC-721 standard, proposed via the Ethereum GitHub, following the launch of various NFT projects that year. The standard coincides with the launch of famous NTF projects like Curio Cards, CryptoPunks, and rare Pepe trading cards. The online game 'CryptoKitties' was made profitable by selling tradable cat NFTs and its success brought public attention to NFTs.

  • Jan-Dec 2020

    The First Patents

    The U.S.A. Patent and Trademark Office receives three trademark applications for NFTs.

  • Mar-Dec 2021

    The Rise of Digital Art

    Interest in NFTs dramatically increases after a number of high-profile sales and art auctions. The number of trademark applications jumpes to more than 1200.

  • Jan 2022

    Towards the Metaverse

    The U.S.A. Patent and Trademark Office receives 450 NFT-related trademark applications. The growing list of brands being trademarked for NFTs includes the NYSE, Star Trek, Panera, Walmart, Elvis Presley, Sports Illustrated, Ticketmaster, and Yahoo.
